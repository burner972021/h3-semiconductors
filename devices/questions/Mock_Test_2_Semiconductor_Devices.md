### Question 1 — PN Junction Electrostatics and Capacitance [22 marks]

A silicon abrupt p-n junction at *T* = 300 K is uniformly doped with *N*<sub>a</sub> = 5 × 10¹⁷ cm⁻³ on the p-side and *N*<sub>d</sub> = 2 × 10¹⁶ cm⁻³ on the n-side. The cross-sectional area of the diode is *A* = 5 × 10⁻⁴ cm².

(a) Sketch a labelled energy band diagram of the junction at thermal equilibrium. Indicate clearly *E*<sub>C</sub>, *E*<sub>V</sub>, *E*<sub>F</sub>, *E*<sub>i</sub>, and the built-in potential *qV*<sub>bi</sub>. **[3 marks]**

(b) Calculate the built-in potential *V*<sub>bi</sub>. **[3 marks]**

(c) Determine the total depletion-region width *W*, the depletion widths *x*<sub>n</sub> and *x*<sub>p</sub>, and the maximum electric field |*E*<sub>max</sub>| at zero applied bias. **[7 marks]**

(d) The diode is then reverse-biased at *V*<sub>R</sub> = 5 V. Calculate the new total junction capacitance *C*<sub>j</sub>. **[5 marks]**

(e) Explain briefly *why* the junction capacitance *decreases* when the magnitude of the reverse bias is increased, while the *storage (diffusion) capacitance* is essentially negligible under reverse bias. **[4 marks]**

### Question 2 — Forward-Biased PN Junction Diode and Breakdown [20 marks]

A long silicon p-n junction diode at 300 K has the following parameters:

| Parameter | p-side | n-side |
|---|---|---|
| Doping | *N*<sub>a</sub> = 1 × 10¹⁷ cm⁻³ | *N*<sub>d</sub> = 5 × 10¹⁵ cm⁻³ |
| Minority carrier diffusion coeff. | *D*<sub>n</sub> = 25 cm²/s | *D*<sub>p</sub> = 10 cm²/s |
| Minority carrier lifetime | *τ*<sub>n</sub> = 5 × 10⁻⁷ s | *τ*<sub>p</sub> = 1 × 10⁻⁷ s |

Cross-sectional area *A* = 10⁻⁴ cm².

(a) Calculate the reverse-saturation current *I*<sub>0</sub> and the forward-bias current when *V*<sub>a</sub> = 0.62 V. **[6 marks]**

(b) Identify which side of the junction dominates the total current under forward bias, and explain *physically* why this is the case. **[4 marks]**

(c) The critical electric field for avalanche breakdown in silicon at this doping range is *E*<sub>c</sub> ≈ 4 × 10⁵ V/cm. Treating the diode as a one-sided p⁺n junction (since *N*<sub>a</sub> ≫ *N*<sub>d</sub>) and neglecting *V*<sub>bi</sub> compared to the breakdown voltage, derive an expression for the breakdown voltage *V*<sub>BR</sub> in terms of *E*<sub>c</sub>, *N*<sub>d</sub>, *q* and *ε*<sub>s</sub>, and hence calculate *V*<sub>BR</sub>. **[6 marks]**

(d) State *one* method by which the breakdown voltage of this diode could be *increased*, and briefly justify your answer. **[4 marks]**

### Question 3 — Metal-Semiconductor Contacts [18 marks]

A metal of work function *qϕ*<sub>m</sub> = 4.75 eV is deposited on n-type silicon (electron affinity *qχ* = 4.01 eV) doped at *N*<sub>d</sub> = 1 × 10¹⁶ cm⁻³ to form an ideal Schottky barrier diode. The Richardson constant *A*\* = 110 A·K⁻²·cm⁻² and the diode area is *A* = 5 × 10⁻⁴ cm².

(a) Determine the Schottky barrier height *qϕ*<sub>B</sub> (from the metal side) and the built-in potential *V*<sub>bi</sub>. **[4 marks]**

(b) Calculate the reverse-saturation current density *J*<sub>sT</sub>, and the forward-bias voltage *V*<sub>a</sub> required to drive a forward current of *I* = 1 mA through the diode. (Neglect the Schottky barrier-lowering effect.) **[6 marks]**

(c) For the *same* forward current of 1 mA, a silicon p-n junction diode of identical area would require a forward-bias voltage of approximately 0.68 V. Without re-calculating it, *explain* why the Schottky diode requires a smaller turn-on voltage and *also* exhibits faster switching than the p-n junction diode. **[4 marks]**

(d) If a *second* metal with *qϕ*<sub>m</sub> = 3.90 eV were deposited on the same n-type silicon, what type of metal-semiconductor contact would result? Sketch the resulting equilibrium energy-band diagram and briefly explain its usefulness in integrated circuits. **[4 marks]**

### Question 4 — Light-Emitting Diode [22 marks]

An n⁺p AlGaAs LED is fabricated. The lightly-doped p-side has *N*<sub>a</sub> = 5 × 10¹⁶ cm⁻³. Other parameters at 300 K are:

- Minority electron diffusion coefficient in the p-region: *D*<sub>n</sub> = 200 cm²/s
- Minority electron lifetime in the p-region: *τ*<sub>n</sub> = 50 ns
- Intrinsic carrier concentration: *n*<sub>i</sub> = 5 × 10⁵ cm⁻³
- Cross-sectional area: *A* = 1 × 10⁻³ cm²
- Refractive index of the semiconductor: *n*<sub>1</sub> = 3.5; *n*<sub>2</sub> = 1 (air)

(a) Briefly explain why direct-bandgap semiconductors (such as AlGaAs and GaAs) are used to fabricate LEDs and laser diodes, while indirect-bandgap silicon is not. **[3 marks]**

(b) The LED is forward-biased at *V*<sub>a</sub> = 1.10 V. Treating it as an n⁺p diode (so that the current is dominated by electron injection into the p-region), calculate the forward current *I*. **[6 marks]**

(c) Assuming an internal radiative recombination efficiency of *η*<sub>r</sub> = 60%, the *external* (extraction) efficiency given by

*F* = (1/4)(*n*<sub>2</sub>/*n*<sub>1</sub>)² · [1 − ((*n*<sub>1</sub> − *n*<sub>2</sub>)/(*n*<sub>1</sub> + *n*<sub>2</sub>))²]

determine the optical output power *P*<sub>opt</sub> emerging from the LED if every emitted photon has energy equal to the bandgap energy *E*<sub>g</sub> = 1.85 eV. **[7 marks]**

(d) Determine the wavelength of the emitted light and identify the colour that would be observed. **[3 marks]**

(e) Suggest *one* reason why the *measured* optical output power is generally lower than the value calculated in part (c), even after accounting for the extraction efficiency. **[3 marks]**

### Question 5 — Photodetector and Conceptual Questions [18 marks]

(a) A long, reverse-biased silicon p-n junction photodiode of cross-sectional area *A* = 1 × 10⁻⁴ cm² has a depletion width *W* = 1.2 μm under operating bias. The minority-carrier diffusion lengths are *L*<sub>n</sub> = 35 μm and *L*<sub>p</sub> = 10 μm. Light of uniform generation rate *G*<sub>L</sub> = 1 × 10²¹ cm⁻³·s⁻¹ is incident on the device. Assuming uniform absorption throughout the structure, calculate the steady-state photocurrent *I*<sub>L</sub>. **[5 marks]**

(b) The result of part (a) consists of a *prompt* component (generated within the depletion region) and a *delayed* component (generated within the neutral regions). Explain the physical origin of the delayed component and state *one* device design modification that would reduce its impact on the speed of response. **[4 marks]**

(c) Sketch and label, on a single set of axes, the *I*–*V* characteristic of a silicon p-n junction diode showing both the **forward-bias** region and the **reverse-bias breakdown** region. Indicate the turn-on voltage and the breakdown voltage. **[3 marks]**

(d) The *built-in potential* of a p-n junction at thermal equilibrium *cannot* be measured by simply connecting a voltmeter across its terminals. Explain why this is the case. **[3 marks]**

(e) Briefly explain the difference between *avalanche breakdown* and *Zener (tunnelling) breakdown* in a reverse-biased p-n junction, and state which mechanism dominates in a *heavily-doped* junction. **[3 marks]**
